Maximilian I, Holy Roman Emperor, attacks the Republic of Venice.
Ferdinand II of Aragon appoints Amerigo Vespucci to the post of Chief Navigator of Spain.
Maximilian I, Holy Roman Emperor, is defeated in Friulia by Venetian forces; he is forced to sign a 3-year truce, and cede several territories to the Republic.
Lebna Dengel succeeds his father Na'od, as Emperor of Ethiopia. Due to his young age, his grandmother Eleni acts as regent.
Michelangelo begins painting the Sistine Chapel ceiling in the Holy See of Rome, on a commission by Pope Julius II (signed May 10).
The League of Cambrai is formed as an alliance against the Republic of Venice, between Pope Julius II, Louis XII of France, Maximilian I, Holy Roman Emperor and Ferdinand II of Aragon.
Portuguese forces under the command of Francisco de Almeida attack Khambhat at the Battle of Dabul.
