Bona Sforza is crowned as Queen of Poland.
A transit of Venus occurs.
Dancing Plague of 1518: A case of dancing mania breaks out in Strasbourg, in which many people die from constant dancing.
Construction of the Manchester Grammar School is completed in England.
The Treaty of London temporarily ensures peace in Western Europe.
A plague of tropical fire ants devastates crops on Hispaniola.
Erasmus publishes his Colloquies.
Henricus Grammateus publishes Ayn neu Kunstlich Buech in Vienna, containing the earliest printed use of plus and minus signs for arithmetic.[1]
The African slave trade begins.
