Christian II is forced to abdicate as King of Denmark and Norway.
Martin Luther helps Katharina von Bora, his eventual wife, escape from the Nimbschen convent, by placing her and 12 other nuns in herring barrels.
The Ningbo Incident: Two rival trade delegations from Japan feud in the Chinese city of Ningbo, resulting in the pillage and plunder of the city.
Gustav Vasa is elected king of Sweden, finally establishing its full independence of Sweden from Denmark, marking the end of the Kalmar Union. This event is also traditionally considered to be the establishment of the modern Swedish nation.
Martin Luther's translation of the Pentateuch into German (Das allte Testament Deutsch) is published.
Johann Esch and Heinrich Voes become the first Flemish Lutheran martyrs, burned at the stake by Roman Catholic authorities in Brussels.
Spanish conquest of Nicaragua: Agreement for an expedition by conquistadores into Nicaragua.
Pope Clement VII succeeds Pope Adrian VI, as the 219th pope.
The Ming dynasty Chinese navy captures two Western ships with Portuguese breech–loading culverins aboard, which the Chinese call a fo–lang–ji (Frankish culverin). According to the Ming Shi, these cannons are soon presented to the Jiajing Emperor by Wang Hong, and their design is copied in 1529.
Wijerd Jelckama, a Frisian warlord and military commander, is executed in Leeuwarden. His death ends the successful Frisian rebellion, fought by the Arumer Black Heap.
Franconian War: The Swabian League destroys 23 robber baron castles.
